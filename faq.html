Frequently Asked Questions

What is Tech Layoff Watch?

Tech Layoff Watch is a free dashboard that tracks tech company layoffs by month, company, and headcount. It visualizes layoff trends with interactive charts and provides a searchable company-by-company table with a running total of affected employees.

Where does the data come from?

Our data is curated from multiple public sources including company press releases, SEC filings, and reputable news organizations like TechCrunch, Bloomberg, and Reuters. Each entry includes a source link for verification.

How often is the data updated?

We aim to update the data monthly at minimum. Major layoff events from well-known companies are typically added within a few days of public announcement.

Can I export the data?

Yes! Click the "Export CSV" button on the main dashboard to download the current filtered dataset as a CSV file that you can open in Excel, Google Sheets, or any spreadsheet application.

How do I search for a specific company?

Use the search bar on the main dashboard. You can search by company name or industry. The chart, table, and statistics will automatically update to show only matching results.

How do I filter by year?

Use the year selector buttons on the dashboard. Click "All Years" to see all data, or select a specific year (2022-2026) to filter events for that year only.

Is this data accurate?

We strive for accuracy by cross-referencing multiple sources. However, layoff numbers can vary between reports and some companies do not disclose exact figures. We always link to our source so you can verify independently.

Can I submit a layoff report?

Currently, we curate data from public sources. If you have information about a layoff event with a credible source, please send it to taeshinkim11@gmail.com and we will review it for inclusion.

Is this service free?

Yes, Tech Layoff Watch is completely free to use. We sustain the service through non-intrusive advertising.

What languages are supported?

The interface is available in 8 languages: English, Korean, Japanese, Chinese (Simplified), Spanish, French, German, and Portuguese. Use the language selector in the top navigation to switch languages.
